3 Techniques to Perfect Your Pixel Art

Beyond simply placing colors, keeping the following tips in mind will help you achieve a more professional pixel art finish.

  • Eliminate "Jaggies": When pixels overlap irregularly at line corners, lines look jagged and messy. Try to place pixels shifting step-by-step diagonally to create smoother lines.
  • Intentionally Limit Your Palette: Restricting the number of colors—similar to retro game consoles—creates a cohesive and authentic "pixel art look." Make good use of the "Save Color" feature in this tool.
  • Anti-aliasing (Intermediate Colors): Placing an intermediate shade between the object border and the background color by just one pixel makes curves look much smoother when zoomed out.

Guide to Choosing Resolutions

  • 16×16 px: Perfect for favicons and micro-icons. It is also an excellent exercise for learning how to express shapes within a strictly limited amount of information.
  • 32×32 px: The most commonly used size for social media profile pictures and retro RPG character walking graphics.
  • 64×64 px or higher: Best for adding complex shading, details, and detailed facial expressions. For beginners, we highly recommend starting with 32×32.

How to Use

  1. Select your canvas size (e.g., 32×32) and click "Reset" to initialize the grid.
  2. Choose your preferred color using the color picker, and draw on the canvas with the Pen tool.
  3. For detailed work, adjust the magnification using the "Zoom" slider or your mouse wheel, and adjust the view position with the "Move" tool.
  4. If you want a transparent background, simply leave the grid unpainted.
  5. Finally, select your preferred export size (32x Zoom is recommended) and click "Save Image" to download your artwork.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q. What are the copyright conditions for the created images?

A. There are no restrictions on the images created with this tool. You can freely use them for both personal and commercial purposes completely free of charge.

Q. The saved image appears blurry. How can I fix this?

A. To preserve the crisp texture of pixel art, choose an enlarged export size like "32x Zoom." Saving at the original size (1:1) may cause the image to look blurry when viewed on high-resolution displays or mobile devices.

Q. Can I resume editing my work later?

A. Yes. By exporting your file using "Save Project (.json)," you can import that JSON file later via "Load JSON File" and resume editing exactly where you left off.
