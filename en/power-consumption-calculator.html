What is the Electricity Cost & Energy Calculator?

This tool allows you to calculate the energy consumed (Joules), electricity usage (Wh/kWh), and estimated costs from an appliance's power rating (W) and duration of use. It's useful for saving electricity, school projects, or household budgeting.

Definitions and Formulas

  • Joule (J): The energy consumed when 1W of power is used for 1 second.
  • Watt-hour (Wh): The energy consumed when 1W of power is used for 1 hour.
  • Kilowatt-hour (kWh): Equal to 1,000Wh; the standard unit used for billing by utility companies.
  • Electricity Cost: Energy (kWh) × Rate (Price per kWh). You can customize the rate in the input field.

Did you know? 1Wh is equivalent to 3,600J because there are 3,600 seconds in one hour.

Key Features

  • Instant Cost Estimates: See the financial impact based on your local electricity rate.
  • Completely Free: Access all features at no cost.
  • No Registration: Start calculating immediately without creating an account.
  • Private & Secure: All calculations are processed locally in your browser. No data is sent to our servers.

How to Use

  1. Enter the Power value and select the unit (W or kW).
  2. Enter the Usage Time and select the unit (seconds, minutes, hours, or days).
  3. Enter your Electricity Rate (Default is set as a sample value).
  4. Results for Joules, Wh/kWh, and Cost will update automatically in real-time.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q. What electricity rate should I enter?

A. You can find your specific rate on your last utility bill or through your provider's website. Common rates vary globally (e.g., around $0.15/kWh in parts of the US).

Q. How much energy is 1kWh?

A. Using a 1000W appliance for 1 hour consumes exactly 1kWh. For example, running a 1000W hair dryer for 1 hour daily results in 30kWh per month.
