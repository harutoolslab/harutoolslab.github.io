What is a 5-Band Resistor Color Code Calculator?

   

        This tool is an online simulator used to instantly calculate resistance (ohms) and tolerance from the color bands on a resistor.         While standard resistors use 4 bands, 5-band metal film resistors are commonly used in precision equipment for higher accuracy.    

   

How to Read and Calculate Values

   

        Resistor color codes represent the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd digits, followed by a multiplier and tolerance band.         Our tool automates the following formula:    

   
           
  • For 5-Band: (1st Digit × 100 + 2nd Digit × 10 + 3rd Digit) × Multiplier
    •    
   

        For example, a "Brown-Black-Black-Red-Brown" 5-band resistor calculates as (1, 0, 0) × 100 = 10,000Ω, which is 10kΩ (±1% tolerance).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

   
      Q. What is the difference between 4-band and 5-band resistors?      

A. 5-band resistors are typically "precision resistors." Having an extra digit band allows for more specific values and tighter tolerances.

   
   
      Q. Which side is the "1st band"?      

A. On most resistors, the tolerance band (5th) is separated from the others by a larger gap. Also, bands like Gold, Silver, or Brown are frequently used as the tolerance end (right side).
